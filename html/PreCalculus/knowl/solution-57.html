Substitute the given information into the slope formula.

We have \(=-\frac{2}{3}\) and we can let \((x_1,y_1)=(6,-3)\) and \((x_2,y_2)=(-9,y)\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} m\amp = \frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}\\ -\frac{2}{3}\amp =\frac{y-(-3)}{-9-6}\\ -\frac{2}{3}\amp =\frac{y+3}{-15}\\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

After substituting in the given information, the only variable left is \(y\text{.}\) Solve.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \alert{-15}\left(-\frac{2}{3}\right)\amp = \alert{-15}\left(-\frac{y+3}{15}\right)\\ 10\amp = y+3\\ 7\amp = y \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

The \(y\)-value which satisfies the conditions above is \(y=7\text{.}\)
