In order to use the logarithm function in applications, it is useful to understand a few properties about the logarithm including its graph, domain, and range. Recall that the logarithm function is the inverse of an exponential function. Therefore, it may be helpful to first consider the graph of the function \(f(x)=e^x\text{.}\)

From our previous understanding of exponential functions, we know that the domain of the function \(f(x)=e^x \) is \((-\infty,\infty)\) and the range is \((0,\infty)\text{.}\) Since the function \(g(x)=\ln(x) \) is the inverse of the function \(f(x)=e^x \) we know that the domain of \(g(x)=\ln(x) \) must be \((0,\infty)\) and the range must be \((-\infty,\infty)\text{.}\) Furthermore, the graph of the function \(g(x)=\ln(x) \) must look like a reflection of the graph \(f(x)=e^x\) reflected through the line \(y=x\text{.}\)
