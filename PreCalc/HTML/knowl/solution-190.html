Solution101.1

Since the two lines are parallel we know that the slope of the new line must be the same as the slope of the original line. That is \(m_1=\frac{-1}{m_2}\text{.}\) We can now use point slope form \(y-(-1)=-\frac{1}{3}(x-4)\text{.}\) At this point we have the equation of a line. However, most people are more comfortable writing lines in slope-intercept form so we will do that here. \(y=-\frac{1}{3}x-\frac{7}{3}\)
