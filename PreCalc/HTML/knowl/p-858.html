An alternate technique for factoring trinomials, called the \(ac\) method, makes use of the grouping method for factoring four-term polynomials. If a trinomial in the form \(ax^2+bx+c\) can be factored, then the middle term, \(bx\text{,}\) can be replaced with two terms with coefficients whose sum is \(b\) and whose product is \(ac\text{.}\) This substitution results in an equivalent expression with four terms that can be factored by grouping.
