The polynomial has degree six, an even number, so its graph starts at the upper left and extends to the upper right. The \(y\)-coordinate of the \(y\)-intercept is

\begin{equation*} f(0) = (2)^3(-1)(-3)^2 = -72. \end{equation*}

Note that \(f\) has a zero of multiplicity three at \(x = -2\text{,}\) a zero of multiplicity one at \(x = 1\text{,}\) and a zero of multiplicity two at \(x = 3\text{.}\) The graph has an S-shaped curve at \(x = -2\text{,}\) crosses the \(x\)-axis at \(x = 1\text{,}\) touches the \(x\)-axis at \(x = 3\text{,}\) and then changes direction, as shown in FigureÂ 327.
