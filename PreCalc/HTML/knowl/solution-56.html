Solution67.1

Given \((−3, −5)\) and \((2, 1)\text{,}\) calculate the difference of the \(y\)-values divided by the difference of the \(x\)-values. Take care to be consistent when subtracting the coordinates:

Let \((x_1,y_1)=(-3,-5)\) and \((x_2,y_2)=(2,1)\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} m\amp = \frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}\\ \amp =\frac{1-(-5)}{2-(-3)}\\ \amp =\frac{1+5}{2+3}\\ \amp =\frac{6}{5} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

The slope is given by \(m=\frac{6}{5}\text{.}\)
