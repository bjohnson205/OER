We begin by writing two sets of blank parentheses. If a trinomial of this form factors, then it will factor into two linear binomial factors.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} x^2+12x+20= (\: \: \: \: )(\: \: \: \: ) \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Write the factors of the first term in the first space of each set of parentheses. In this case, factor \(x^2=x\cdot x\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} x^2+12+20=(x\: \: \: \: )(x\: \: \: \: ) \end{equation*}

Determine the factors of the last term whose sum equals the coefficient of the middle term. To do this, list all of the factorizations of 20 and search for factors whose sum equals \(12\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} 20\amp = 1\cdot 20 \: \rightarrow \: 1 +20=21\\ \amp = 2\cdot 10 \: \rightarrow \: 2 +10=12\\ \amp = 4\cdot 5 \: \rightarrow \: 4 +5=9 \end{align*}

Choose \(20 = 2 \cdot 10\) because \(2 + 10 = 12\text{.}\) We then write in the last term of each binomial using the factors determined in the previous step.

\begin{equation*} x^2+12x+20=(x+2)(x+10) \end{equation*}

We check by multiplying the two binomials.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} (x+2)(x+10)\amp =x^2+10x+2x+20\\ \amp = x^2+12x+20 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
