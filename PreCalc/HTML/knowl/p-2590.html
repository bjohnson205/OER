\begin{equation*} f(t)=2\cos\left(\frac{\pi}{4}(t)\right)+1\text{.} \end{equation*}

Note that the period, midline and amplitude of both functions is different. Thus, the transformed graph involves a vertical shift up by 1, a vertical stretch of 2, and a horizontal stretch by a factor of \(4/\pi\) from the original graph of \(g(t)=\cos(t)\text{,}\) which we discussed in SectionÂ .
