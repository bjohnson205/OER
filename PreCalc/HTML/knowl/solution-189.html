Solution100.1

Since the two lines are parallel we know that the slope of the new line must be the same as the slope of the original line. That is \(m_1=3=m_2\text{.}\) We can now use point slope form \(y-(-1)=3(x-4)\text{.}\) At this point we have the equation of a line. However, most people are more comfortable writing lines in slope-intercept form so we will do that here. \(y=3x-13\)
