Quadratic functions, such as \(f(x) = -2x^2 + 6x + 8\text{,}\) are polynomials of degree \(2\text{.}\) The graph of every quadratic function is a parabola, with the same basic shape as the standard parabola, \(y = x^2\text{.}\) (See FigureÂ 314.) Notice that the graph changes from increasing to decreasing or vice versa at one point. This point of a parabola is the same as its vertex (which we defined in section 10.2).
