Convert \(3.75\)% to a decimal number before using it in the formula:

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} r=3.75\text{%}=0.0375 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Use this and the fact that \(p=$1250\) and \(t=2\) years to calculate the simple interest:

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} I \amp = prt\\ \amp = (\alert{1250})(\alert{0.0375})(\alert{2})\\ \amp = 93.75 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

The simple interest earned is \($93.75\text{.}\)
