We can solve for either variable in either equation. Since the first equation has a term with coefficient \(1\text{,}\) we choose to solve for that first.

\begin{align*} -5x+y\amp = -1\\ y\amp = 5x-1 \end{align*}

Substitute into the other equation.

\begin{align*} 10x-2y\amp =2\\ 10x-2(\alert{5x-1})\amp =2\\ 10x-10x+2\amp = 2\\ 2\amp = 2 \end{align*}

In this case, our variables cancelled and we ended up with a true statement. This means the equation is an identity and any real number is a solution. The solutions are of the form \((x,mx+b)\text{,}\) and specifically in this case, our solutions are \((x,5x-1)\text{,}\) where \(x\) is any real number.
