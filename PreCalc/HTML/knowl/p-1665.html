Looking at our compound interest formula

\begin{equation*} A(t) = P(1 + \dfrac{r}{n})^{nt}, \end{equation*}

we know that we are compounding twice a year, so \(n=2\text{,}\) and the nominal rate is \(24\)%, so \(r=.24\text{.}\) The problem also tells us the original price of a pound of butter was $\(2\text{,}\) so \(P=2\text{.}\) Using this information, we get the formula

\begin{equation*} A(t) = 2(1 + \dfrac{.24}{2})^{2t}= 2(1.12)^{2t}. \end{equation*}
