In this situation, revolving about \(y = 4\text{,}\) we see that the outer radius of a typical washer is \(R(x) = 4 - x^3\text{,}\) while the inner radius is \(r(x) = 4 - 2x\text{,}\) and thus the volume is

\begin{equation*} V = \int_{0}^{\sqrt{2}} \pi ( (4 - x^3)^2 - (4-2x)^2 ) \, dx = \left( 8-\frac{32\sqrt{2}}{21} \right)\pi \approx 18.3626\text{.} \end{equation*}
