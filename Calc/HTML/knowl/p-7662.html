In the special case where \(a=0\) in Definition 8.24, the Taylor series is also called the Maclaurin series for \(f\text{.}\) From Example 8.22 we know the \(n\)th order Taylor polynomial centered at \(0\) for the exponential function \(e^x\text{;}\) thus, the Maclaurin series for \(e^x\) is

\begin{equation*} \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^k}{k!}\text{.} \end{equation*}
