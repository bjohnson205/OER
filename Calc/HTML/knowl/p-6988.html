The sum in (8.8) is a partial sum of the geometric series with ratio \(1+\frac{r}{12}\text{.}\) So formula (8.4) shows that the partial sum \(\displaystyle \sum_{k=0}^{n-1} \left(1+\frac{r}{12}\right)^k\) is equal to

\begin{align*} \displaystyle \sum_{k=0}^{n-1} \left(1+\frac{r}{12}\right)^k \amp = \frac{1-\left(1+\frac{r}{12}\right)^n}{1-\left(1+\frac{r}{12}\right)}\\ \amp = \left(\frac{12}{r}\right) \left(\left(1+\frac{r}{12}\right)^n - 1\right)\text{.} \end{align*}
