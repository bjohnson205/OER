Let's consider the familiar differential equation from exponential population growth given by

\begin{equation} y' = ky\text{,}\tag{8.25} \end{equation}

where \(k\) is the constant of proportionality. While we can solve this differential equation using methods we have already learned, we take a different approach now that can be applied to a much larger set of differential equations. For the rest of this activity, let's assume that \(k=1\text{.}\) We will use our knowledge of Taylor series to find a solution to the differential equation (8.25).
