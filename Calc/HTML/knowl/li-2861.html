Letting \(F(x) = \int_4^x \sin(t^2) \, dt\) it follows that we need to compute \(\frac{d}{dx} \left[F(x^3)] \right]\text{.}\) By the Chain Rule, \(\frac{d}{dx} \left[F(x^3)] \right] = F'(x^3) \cdot 3x^2\text{.}\) By the Second FTC, we know that \(F'(x) = \sin(x^2)\text{,}\) and thus \(\frac{d}{dx} \left[ \int_4^{x^3} \sin(t^2) \, dt \right] = \frac{d}{dx} \left[F(x^3)] \right] = \sin(x^6) \cdot 3x^2\text{.}\)
