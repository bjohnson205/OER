On the first roll, the probability of not rolling a \(6\) is \(\frac{5}{6}\text{.}\) This is the same on the second roll, so the probability of not rolling a \(6\) on either of the first two rolls is \(\frac{5}{6} \cdot \frac{5}{6}\text{,}\) since the two events are independent. If we then roll a \(6\) on the following turn, the probability of doing so in that roll is \(\frac{1}{6}\text{,}\) so with the three rolls being independent, the probability of you rolling a \(6\) on your second turn (the third overall roll) is

\begin{equation*} P = \left( \frac{5}{6} \right) \left( \frac{5}{6} \right) \left( \frac{1}{6} \right)\text{.} \end{equation*}
