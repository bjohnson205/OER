Slicing perpendicular to the axis of rotation, we get slices of thickness \(\Delta y\) that are shaped like washers. The outer radius, \(R(y)\text{,}\) is given by \(R(y) = 4\text{;}\) the innner radius is given by the function \(y = \sqrt{x}\text{,}\) but expressed with \(x\) as a function of \(y\text{,}\) so \(r(y) = y^2\text{.}\) It follows that a representative washer slice has volume

\begin{equation*} V_{\text{slice}} = \pi (R(y)^2 - r(y)^2) \Delta y = \pi (16 - y^4) \Delta y\text{.} \end{equation*}

Further, since \(y = \sqrt{x}\) and \(x = 4\) intersect at the point \((4,2)\text{,}\) we integrate from \(y = 0\) to \(y = 2\) and thus find that the total volume is

\begin{equation*} V = \int_0^2 \pi (16 - y^4) \, dy\text{.} \end{equation*}
