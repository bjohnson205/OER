It is possible that an \(n\)th order Taylor polynomial is not a polynomial of degree \(n\text{;}\) that is, the order of the approximation can be different from the degree of the polynomial. For example, in ActivityÂ 8.28 we found that the second order Taylor polynomial \(P_2(x)\) centered at \(0\) for \(\sin(x)\) is \(P_2(x) = x\text{.}\) In this case, the second order Taylor polynomial is a degree 1 polynomial.
