Solution5.8.1

  1. By the Second FTC, \(\frac{d}{dx} \left[ \int_4^x e^{t^2} \, dt \right] = e^{x^2}\text{.}\)

  2. By the First FTC, \(\int_{-2}^x \frac{d}{dt} \left[ \frac{t^4}{1+t^4} \right] \, dt = \left. \left[ \frac{t^4}{1+t^4} \right] \right|_{-2}^{x} = \frac{x^4}{1+x^4} - \frac{8}{9}\text{.}\)

  3. Since \(\int_{x}^{1} g(t) \, dt = -\int_{1}^{x} g(t) \, dt\text{,}\) it follows by this fact and the Second FTC that \(\frac{d}{dx} \left[ \int_{x}^1 \cos(t^3) \, dt \right] = -\frac{d}{dx} \left[ \int_{1}^x \cos(t^3) \, dt \right] = -\cos(x^3)\text{.}\)

  4. By the First FTC, \(\int_{3}^x \frac{d}{dt} \left[ \ln(1+t^2) \right] \, dt = \left. \ln(1+t^2) \right|_{3}^{x} = \ln(1+x^2)-\ln(4)\text{.}\)

  5. Letting \(F(x) = \int_4^x \sin(t^2) \, dt\) it follows that we need to compute \(\frac{d}{dx} \left[F(x^3)] \right]\text{.}\) By the Chain Rule, \(\frac{d}{dx} \left[F(x^3)] \right] = F'(x^3) \cdot 3x^2\text{.}\) By the Second FTC, we know that \(F'(x) = \sin(x^2)\text{,}\) and thus \(\frac{d}{dx} \left[ \int_4^{x^3} \sin(t^2) \, dt \right] = \frac{d}{dx} \left[F(x^3)] \right] = \sin(x^6) \cdot 3x^2\text{.}\)
