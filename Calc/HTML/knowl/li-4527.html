A plot of the first 50 terms of the sequence \(\left\{\frac{5+3^n}{10+2^n}\right\}\) is shown here.

The plot implies that the sequence does not have a limit as \(n\) goes to infinity. For large \(n\) the \(3^n\) term dominates the numerator and the \(2^n\) term the denominator. So \(\frac{5+3^n}{10+2^n}\) looks like \(\frac{3^n}{2^n} = \left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^n\) when \(n\) is big. Since \(\frac{3}{2} \gt 1\text{,}\) the sequence \(\left\{\frac{5+3^n}{10+2^n}\right\}\) diverges to infinity.
