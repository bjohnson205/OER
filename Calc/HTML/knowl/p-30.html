Electronic Edition

Because students and instructors alike have access to the book in electronic format, there are several advantages to the text over a traditional print text. One is that the text may be projected on a screen in the classroom (or even better, on a whiteboard) and the instructor may reference ideas in the text directly, add comments or notation or features to graphs, and indeed write right on the text itself. Students can do likewise, choosing to print only whatever portions of the text are needed for them. In addition, the electronic versions of the text includes live .html links to java applets, so student and instructor alike may follow those links to additional resources that lie outside the text itself. Finally, students can have access to a copy of the text anywhere they have internet access. The .html version is far superior to the .pdf version; this is especially true for viewing on a smartphone. It is worthwhile to download the .pdf version for local access in the event of internet issues.

Note. In the .pdf version, there is not an obvious visual indicator of the live .html links, so some available information is suppressed. If you are using the text electronically in a setting with internet access, please know that it is assumed you are using the .html version.

Activities Workbook

Each section of the text has a preview activity and typically at least three in-class activities embedded in the discussion. As it is the expectation that students will complete all of these activities, it is ideal for them to have room to work on them adjacent to the problem statements themselves. As a separate document, we have compiled a workbook of activities that includes only the individual activity prompts, along with space provided for students to write their responses. This workbook is the one printing expense that students will almost certainly have to undertake, and is available in .pdf upon request, as well as for purchase for a nominal fee on Amazon.

Answers

In the 2018 edition, we now offer answers to non- WeBWorK exercises and all activities other than the previews. To save printing cost, the answers are only available in the .html and .pdf versions of the text, not in the print version for sale on Amazon. Anyone using the print version should refer to the appendices in an electronic version for access to answers.

Community of Users

Because this text is free and open-source, we hope that as people use the text, they will contribute corrections, suggestions, and new material. The best way to communicate such feedback is by email to Matt Boelkins at boelkinm at gvsu dot edu. We also have a blog at opencalculus.wordpress.com, where we post new developments, other free resources, feedback, and other points of discussion.
