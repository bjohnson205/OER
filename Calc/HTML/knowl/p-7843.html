Assume that we can differentiate a power series term by term. By taking the derivative of \(f(x)\) with respect to \(x\) and substituting the result into the differential equation (8.25), show that the equation

\begin{equation*} \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} ka_kx^{k-1} = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} a_kx^{k} \end{equation*}

must be satisfied in order for \(f(x) = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} a_kx^k\) to be a solution of the DE.
