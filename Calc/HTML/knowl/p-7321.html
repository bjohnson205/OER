  1. For any \(k\) we have \(k \gt k-1\text{,}\) so if \(k \gt 1\) then it follows that \(0 \lt \frac{1}{k} \lt \frac{1}{k-1}\text{.}\) Since the harmonic series \(\sum \frac{1}{k}\) diverges, the Direct Comparison Test shows that the larger series \(\sum \frac{1}{k-1}\) must also diverge.

  2. In this case we know that \(k^3 \lt k^3+1\) and so if \(k \gt 0\) then \(\frac{1}{k^3} \gt \frac{1}{k^3+1}\text{.}\) It follows that \(\frac{k}{k^3} \gt \frac{k}{k^3+1}\) and so the series \(\sum \frac{k}{k^3+1}\) is bounded below by 0 and above by the series \(\sum \frac{k}{k^3}\text{.}\) Now

    \begin{equation*} \frac{k}{k^3} = \frac{1}{k^2} \end{equation*}

    and so the series \(\sum \frac{k}{k^3}\) is a \(p\)-series with \(p=2\) and converges. The Direct Comparison Test shows that the smaller series \(\sum \frac{k}{k^3+1}\) must also converge.
